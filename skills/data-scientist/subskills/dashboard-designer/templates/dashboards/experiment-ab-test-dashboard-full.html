Risks, Caveats & Assumptions

Known limitations and uncertainty sources that affect interpretation of these results.

Unresolved Units
1,079 units (0.9%) pending outcome resolution

Units assigned within the experiment window but whose observation window has not yet closed. Excluded from all rate calculations. Low impact given sample size.

Sample Imbalance
Segment D allocation skew (−108, −1.6%)

Within pre-registered tolerance of ±2%. Covariate balance checks pass. Low risk of confounding, but segment-level estimates for Segment D should carry wider error bars.

Monitoring Window
Single experiment window may not capture seasonality

The experiment ran within one contiguous window. Effects that vary by time-of-period, day-of-week, or external events are not captured. Holdout validation recommended.

Guardrail Uncertainty
Guardrail Metric A: nominal significance, small effect

At p = 0.031 without multiplicity correction, the guardrail signal may be a false positive. Effect size (+0.3 pp) is well below the severity threshold. Continue monitoring.

Missing Exposure Records
2.2% of assigned units have incomplete exposure logs

1,397 treatment and 1,519 control units with partial or unverifiable exposure records. Balanced across arms. Excluded from per-protocol sensitivity analysis (not shown).

Multiplicity
No multiplicity correction applied (5 metrics pre-registered)

Primary metric is the single decision metric. Secondary, guardrail, and quality metrics are monitored but do not control the shipment decision. Guardrail significance should be interpreted with caution.

Segment Concentration
Small samples in Segments D, F, and G

Segment G (N = 7,010) and Segment F (N = 10,780) have limited sample for segment-level inference. Segment D has both small N and allocation skew. Overall effect is robust to segment exclusion.

  3. 3

    Retain 5% of the population as a long-term holdout from the control arm for 90-day persistence measurement. This provides an untreated baseline to detect novelty effects, seasonality confounds, or delayed degradation.

  4. 4
    Archive experiment metadata and assumptions.

    Register experiment ID, seed, population definition, assignment method, exclusion rules, pre-registered metrics, thresholds, and analysis notebook in the experiment registry before ramp begins.

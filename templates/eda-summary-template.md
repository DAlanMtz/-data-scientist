# EDA Summary Template

## Purpose

Communicate the key findings from exploratory analysis as a structured report artifact — what the target looks like, which features are candidate signals, what anomalies exist, and what hypotheses are supported or refuted. This is the *summary deliverable* from EDA, not the notebook itself.

> **Relationship to `eda-notebook-template.md`:** That template guides the *process* of doing EDA in a notebook. This template structures the *report or summary* produced at the end of Stage 2 — the artifact that informs modeling decisions and can be shared with stakeholders.

## When to Use

At the end of **Stage 2: Exploratory Analysis**. May be a structured Markdown document, a slide deck section, or an annotated notebook summary. Produce it before finalizing the validation plan or feature list.

## Required Inputs

- Data Audit from Stage 1
- Project Brief from Stage 0
- EDA notebook or analysis outputs (distributions, relationships, quality checks)

## Minimum Contents

### 1. Summary

- Project: `[name]`
- EDA date: `[date]`
- Analyst: `[name]`
- Data snapshot: `[date range and row count analyzed]`
- Key findings in 3–5 bullets:
  - `[Finding 1]`
  - `[Finding 2]`
  - `[Finding 3]`

### 2. Target Variable Profile

| Dimension | Result |
|---|---|
| Overall prevalence / mean / distribution | `[value]` |
| Trend over time | `[stable / seasonal / shifting — describe]` |
| By top segment | `[e.g., segment A: 12%, segment B: 4%]` |
| Label quality concerns | `[none / describe]` |
| Class imbalance | `[ratio / describe]` |

Include at minimum: overall distribution and at least one time-based and one segment-based view.

### 3. Candidate Signal Summary

| Feature | Relationship to target | Strength | Leakage risk | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[feature]` | `[positive/negative/nonlinear/none]` | `[strong/moderate/weak]` | `[none/low/review]` | `[hypothesis]` |

List the top 5–10 candidates. Note any features that appeared promising but were ruled out and why.

### 4. Anomaly and Outlier Review

| Observation | Fields affected | Likely cause | Disposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| `[description]` | `[fields]` | `[data error / real variation / collection artifact]` | `[exclude / keep / flag / investigate]` |

### 5. Hypothesis Log

For each hypothesis generated by EDA:

| # | Hypothesis | Supporting evidence | Contradicting evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `[hypothesis]` | `[evidence]` | `[evidence or none]` | `[supported / refuted / open]` |

Minimum: 3 hypotheses. A supported hypothesis informs feature engineering. A refuted hypothesis prevents a wasted modeling detour.

### 6. Modeling Readiness Assessment

- Target is valid and well-defined: `[ ] Yes / [ ] No — [issue]`
- Enough rows and positive cases: `[ ] Yes / [ ] No — [issue]`
- Reliable split possible (time-aware or group-aware if needed): `[ ] Yes / [ ] No — [issue]`
- Key features available at prediction time: `[ ] Yes / [ ] No — [issue]`
- Data quality risks are manageable: `[ ] Yes / [ ] No — [issue]`
- Overall readiness: `[ ] Ready` / `[ ] Conditionally ready` / `[ ] Not ready — [blocker]`

## Output Format

Structured Markdown document or slide section. Should stand alone — a stakeholder who did not run the notebook should be able to read the EDA Summary and understand what the data supports, what it does not, and what the next steps are.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Target distribution analyzed overall, over time, and across at least one segment
- [ ] At least 3 candidate signals documented with evidence
- [ ] At least one anomaly reviewed and dispositioned
- [ ] At least 3 hypotheses logged with supporting or contradicting evidence
- [ ] Modeling readiness verdict is explicit — not left as an open question
- [ ] No candidate features included that have known leakage risk without a documented disposition

## Common Mistakes

- EDA summary is a collection of charts without interpretation — every finding needs a business or modeling implication
- Only the aggregate distribution is shown — no time or segment breakdown
- Hypotheses not documented — EDA becomes decoration rather than input to modeling decisions
- Promising features are included without checking prediction-time availability
- Anomalies are noted in the notebook but not surfaced in the summary
